🇵🇭 Philippines
Geographic Risk Profile

Extremely high disaster risk.

Reasons:

• located in Pacific typhoon belt
• volcanic arc
• earthquake faults
• mountainous islands prone to landslides

Average:

20 tropical storms per year

Major Historical Disasters
Super Typhoon Haiyan (2013)

One of the strongest storms ever recorded.

Impact:

• winds over 315 km/h
• 6,300+ deaths
• 4 million displaced

Cities devastated:

Tacloban
Leyte
Eastern Visayas

Mount Pinatubo Eruption (1991)

One of the largest volcanic eruptions of the 20th century.

Impact:

• 800+ deaths
• massive ash cloud
• global temperature drop

2025 Typhoon Season

Multiple storms including Ragasa, Kalmaegi, and Fung-wong hit the country, killing hundreds and flooding major islands.

Current Disaster Pattern

Most common hazards:

• super typhoons
• storm surges
• flooding
• landslides
• volcanic eruptions

Future Forecast Risks

Climate models predict:

• stronger typhoons
• heavier rainfall
• larger storm surges

Major vulnerable cities:

Manila
Cebu
Davao

🇮🇩 Indonesia
Geographic Risk Profile

One of the most geologically active countries in the world.

Located along the Pacific Ring of Fire.

Hazards:

• earthquakes
• volcanoes
• tsunamis
• floods
• landslides

Indonesia has 130+ active volcanoes.

Major Historical Disasters
2004 Indian Ocean Tsunami

Triggered by a massive earthquake near Sumatra.

Impact:

• 230,000 deaths globally
• 170,000 in Indonesia alone

Mount Merapi Eruption (2010)

Indonesia’s most active volcano.

Impact:

• 350+ deaths
• thousands evacuated

2025 Sumatra Flood Disaster

Severe flooding and landslides killed over 1,000 people and displaced more than a million residents.

Current Disaster Pattern

Frequent hazards:

• volcanic eruptions
• earthquakes
• monsoon flooding
• landslides

Future Forecast Risks

Climate change increases:

• extreme rainfall
• landslides
• coastal flooding

High-risk regions:

Java
Sumatra
Sulawesi

🇹🇭 Thailand
Geographic Risk Profile

Thailand faces mainly:

• monsoon floods
• droughts

Large rivers increase flood risk.

One of the worst floods in world history.

Impact:

• 815 deaths
• $46 billion economic damage

Bangkok almost completely flooded.

2016–2017 Southern Thailand Floods

Worst rainfall in 30 years, affecting more than 1.8 million people.

Current Disaster Pattern

Common disasters:

• seasonal flooding
• drought in northeast
• tropical storms

Future Forecast Risks

Major concern:

Bangkok sinking

Predictions:

• sea-level rise
• river flooding
• urban drainage failure
